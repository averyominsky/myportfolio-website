Hi, I'm Avery Ominsky.

Design has always been the place where craft and curiosity meet for me — whether that's a museum exhibit someone walks through, a character that has to read instantly from silhouette, or a print piece that only works because of one small typographic decision. I like moving between those registers.

As a designer, I get energy from the parts of the process that are genuinely creative — visual systems, interaction, storytelling. At the same time, I think good designers stay adaptable and comfortable wearing a few different hats. Lately I've been especially interested in how physical craft and screen-based interaction can hold the same idea, and how digital experiences can feel a little warmer and more considered.

Outside of client work, I keep an active illustration and printmaking practice — etchings, monotypes, pen and ink, the occasional cardboard sculpture. I'm always collecting reference from things that catch my eye, whether that's a Studio 54 photo archive or a stranger's outfit on the train.

I'm open to work. If you're hiring, reach out via LinkedIn or email. Or — just say hi.
